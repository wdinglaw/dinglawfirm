Family law disputes can move quickly, especially when a client has been served with papers, needs temporary orders, or must make immediate decisions about parenting, support, or housing. The Law Offices of Wenyu Ding provides focused California family law representation for clients in San Jose and throughout Santa Clara County.

The firm uses a measured, document-driven approach. Each matter is evaluated for deadlines, court posture, negotiation options, and the facts that should be developed before positions are taken.

How We Help

Services include filing or responding to divorce papers, preparing custody and parenting schedule requests, analyzing child support and spousal support issues, organizing property and debt division, and drafting or reviewing settlement agreements.

When court involvement is needed, the firm helps prepare for hearings, temporary orders, declarations, exhibits, and the practical decisions that arise before and after a hearing.

Common Issues Handled

  • Divorce petitions, responses, and early case planning.
  • Custody and parenting schedules, including holiday and vacation terms.
  • Child support, spousal support, and income documentation.
  • Community property, separate property, debts, and settlement terms.
  • Domestic violence restraining order requests or responses.

What to Expect in the Process

The process usually begins by identifying what has already been filed, what must be filed next, and whether immediate relief is needed. Clients should expect requests for court papers, income documents, property records, messages, timelines, and any prior agreements.

Depending on the facts, the next step may be settlement discussion, mediation preparation, discovery, temporary orders, disclosure exchange, or trial-readiness planning. The firm keeps the focus on practical choices and clear documentation.

San Jose family law matters may involve Santa Clara County filing procedures, court calendars, local mediation practices, and Bay Area financial realities such as equity compensation, high housing costs, and dual-career parenting schedules.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the firm help me file or respond to divorce papers?

Yes. The firm assists with filing and response strategy, procedural planning, disclosures, temporary orders, and settlement or litigation next steps.

What if custody and support are both disputed?

Custody schedules and support can affect each other. The firm evaluates parenting time, income, expenses, and practical logistics together.

Do family law cases always require a court hearing?

No. Some matters resolve by agreement, but hearings may be needed for temporary orders, disputed custody, support, DVRO, or enforcement issues.

What is the consultation meant to accomplish?

The consultation is designed to identify issues, deadlines, available documents, and practical next steps. It does not create an attorney-client relationship by itself.
