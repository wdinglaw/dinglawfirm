Structured custody advocacy for serious parenting disputes.

High-conflict custody matters require calm organization, evidence review, careful court preparation, and parenting-plan terms that address real-world communication and safety concerns.

Some custody disputes require more than a basic parenting schedule. Communication breakdowns, repeated schedule problems, safety concerns, urgent parenting issues, and overlapping domestic violence restraining order questions can make a case difficult to manage without a clear plan.

The Law Offices of Wenyu Ding helps San Jose and Santa Clara County parents organize the facts, evidence, timelines, and requested orders needed for serious custody disputes. The firm avoids inflammatory labels and focuses on documented conduct, child-centered logistics, and courtroom-ready preparation.

How We Help

Representation may include emergency issue assessment, custody request or response preparation, parenting-plan structure, hearing preparation, evidence organization, and coordination of custody issues with support, DVRO, or divorce proceedings.

Where settlement is appropriate, the firm works toward clear schedule terms and communication rules. Where court intervention is necessary, preparation focuses on concise requests, organized exhibits, and credible explanations.

Common Issues Handled

  • Communication breakdowns and repeated refusal to follow parenting schedules.
  • Safety concerns, emergency issues, and domestic violence overlap where relevant.
  • Substance abuse allegations when supported by facts and appropriate evidence.
  • Gatekeeping or alienation-type concerns described carefully through documented conduct rather than diagnosis.
  • Evidence organization, hearing preparation, and parenting-plan structure.

What to Expect in the Process

The process often begins with current orders, recent communication, calendars, school or daycare information, safety-related documents, and a timeline of recurring problems. The firm evaluates what must be addressed immediately and what should be handled through negotiation, mediation, or a noticed court request.

Clients should expect careful discussion of what evidence is relevant, what details may distract from the requested orders, and how proposed parenting terms would function after the hearing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a custody case high conflict?

A custody matter may become high conflict when communication breaks down, schedules are not followed, safety concerns arise, or urgent court intervention may be needed.

Can evidence help in a custody dispute?

Evidence can be important when it is relevant, organized, and connected to the requested orders. Messages, calendars, school records, and prior orders may matter depending on the facts.

How should safety concerns be handled?

Safety concerns should be reviewed promptly and carefully. Depending on the facts, custody orders, emergency requests, or restraining order issues may need to be considered.

Does the firm diagnose parental behavior?

No. The firm avoids diagnostic labels and focuses on documented conduct, practical parenting-plan structure, and the legal issues before the court.

Can the firm promise a custody result?

No. Custody outcomes depend on the facts, evidence, legal standards, and court evaluation. The firm focuses on preparation and practical advocacy.
