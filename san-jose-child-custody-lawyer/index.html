What to Expect in the Process

Custody work often begins with the existing orders, the current schedule, the child's needs, communication history, and the requested change. The firm may ask for calendars, school information, messages, prior agreements, police or medical records when relevant, and a concise timeline.

Depending on the issue, the process may involve mediation, a request for temporary orders, responsive declarations, settlement proposals, or a more detailed evidentiary record. Best-interest considerations guide the strategy, but the facts must be presented clearly.

Local Santa Clara County Relevance

San Jose parenting plans should account for commute patterns, school schedules, childcare, work demands, extracurricular activities, and communication tools that help parents reduce avoidable conflict. Local realities matter because a custody order must be workable after the hearing ends.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between legal and physical custody?

Legal custody generally concerns major decision-making. Physical custody generally concerns where the child lives and the parenting schedule.

Can custody orders be modified?

Custody orders may be modified when legally appropriate. The analysis depends on the current order, the facts, and the child's best interests.

What should a parenting schedule address?

A parenting schedule often addresses regular time, holidays, vacations, transportation, communication, school logistics, and procedures for resolving disputes.

Can the firm promise a custody outcome?

No. Custody outcomes depend on the facts, evidence, legal standards, and court evaluation. The firm focuses on preparation and practical advocacy.
