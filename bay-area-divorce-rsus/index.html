Bay Area Divorce Involving RSUs

Divorce guidance for equity compensation issues.

Restricted stock units, stock options, vesting schedules, and tech compensation can affect property division, support, tax planning, and settlement structure in California divorce.

Schedule a ConsultationRSU Insight

Many Bay Area divorce cases involve compensation that is more complex than base salary. RSUs, stock options, bonuses, refresh grants, deferred compensation, and liquidity events can affect both property division and support analysis.

The Law Offices of Wenyu Ding helps clients identify the documents, dates, and settlement terms needed to evaluate equity compensation in a California divorce. The firm does not provide tax advice, but it helps clients understand where legal, tax, and financial review may be needed.

How We Help

Representation may include review of equity award agreements, vesting schedules, grant notices, stock plan documents, paystubs, W-2s, tax records, date-of-separation facts, and proposed settlement language.

The firm evaluates how RSUs or stock options may relate to community property, separate property, income available for support, withholding, valuation, and practical division mechanics.

Common Issues Handled

  • Restricted stock units, stock options, and vesting schedules.
  • Community and separate property characterization.
  • Date-of-separation issues and grant-purpose questions.
  • Income available for child support or spousal support.
  • Tax considerations, settlement structure, and need for legal, tax, or financial review.

What to Expect in the Process

The process starts with collecting compensation records and identifying what has vested, what remains unvested, when grants were made, and why the employer awarded them. These facts can matter for both property characterization and support discussions.

Settlement planning may need to address timing of vesting, tax withholding, transfer limitations, who bears risk of market movement, and how future events will be documented. Clear drafting can reduce later disputes.

Local Bay Area Relevance

San Jose, Milpitas, Santa Clara County, and broader Bay Area divorces often involve tech employees, executives, founders, or dual-income households with variable compensation. These cases benefit from early organization of records rather than waiting until settlement is nearly complete.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are RSUs property, income, or both in divorce?

RSUs may raise both property division and support questions. The analysis depends on vesting, grant purpose, timing, date of separation, and the specific facts.

Why does the date of separation matter?

The date of separation can affect characterization of compensation and property rights. Its impact should be reviewed with counsel based on the records.

Should tax issues be reviewed separately?

Yes. Tax consequences can be significant, and clients should consider legal, tax, and financial review before finalizing settlement terms.

Can settlement terms address future vesting?

Settlement terms may address future vesting, division mechanics, withholding, valuation, and transfer issues, but the language should be carefully drafted.
