High-Asset Divorce Lawyer San Jose

Divorce strategy for complex South Bay finances.

High-asset divorce can involve real estate, separate property claims, community property characterization, RSUs, business interests, retirement accounts, reimbursement claims, support implications, and tax-sensitive settlement structure.

How We Help

The firm assists with document organization, preliminary disclosure planning, separate property and community property analysis, support implications, settlement structure, and preparation for court when disputed issues require intervention.

Common Issues Handled

  • Real estate, refinancing history, title issues, and reimbursement claims.
  • Separate property claims and community property characterization.
  • RSUs, stock options, bonuses, professional income, and support implications.
  • Business interests, retirement accounts, and valuation questions.
  • Settlement structure, judgment terms, and post-judgment implementation.

What to Expect in the Process

The process usually begins with an asset and debt map, income records, tax returns, title documents, account statements, equity award records, and a timeline of acquisition, separation, and major financial events.

From there, the strategy may involve disclosure exchange, informal document requests, formal discovery, negotiation, mediation, temporary orders, or targeted litigation. Strong preparation supports both settlement and court presentation.

Local Santa Clara County Relevance

San Jose high-asset divorce matters often involve Bay Area housing costs, tech compensation, dual-income households, startup or business interests, and assets acquired before and during marriage. These issues should be reviewed early so settlement terms are not built on incomplete assumptions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What issues make a divorce financially complex?

Financial complexity may involve real estate, separate property claims, business interests, equity compensation, retirement accounts, reimbursement claims, or support implications.

How are RSUs addressed in a high-asset divorce?

RSUs may raise property division, income, support, vesting, and tax questions. The analysis depends on the grant documents, timing, purpose, and settlement structure.

Are tax professionals sometimes needed?

Yes. Some matters benefit from tax, financial, valuation, or forensic accounting input. The need depends on the assets and disputed issues.

Can high-asset cases settle?

Yes. Many financially complex cases can resolve by agreement when disclosures are complete, values are supported, and settlement terms are clearly drafted.
