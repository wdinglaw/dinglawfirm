Can Text Messages Be Used in California Family Court?

Text messages can matter in family court, but they are not automatically useful just because they are upsetting. The key questions are relevance, context, authenticity, and how the messages connect to the relief requested.

By Law Offices of Wenyu Ding. This article is general information, not legal advice.

Relevance comes first

Messages should relate to a legal issue such as custody, support, threats, harassment, disclosure, exchanges, or compliance with orders.

Context matters

A single screenshot can be misleading. Courts often need enough surrounding context to understand who said what, when, and why it matters.

Frequently asked questions

Are screenshots enough?

Sometimes screenshots are used, but authentication and context may still be challenged.

Should I respond to hostile messages?

Often a brief, calm, issue-focused response is better than escalation, but strategy depends on the case.

Can social media messages be used too?

Potentially, if they are relevant and properly preserved.
