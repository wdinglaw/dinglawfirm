Santa Clara County Divorce Attorney

Wenyu Ding, Esq.

Wenyu Ding is the founder of the Law Offices of Wenyu Ding, a Bay Area family law practice based in Milpitas and serving clients throughout Santa Clara County, San Jose, and Northern California. His practice focuses on divorce, child custody, child support, spousal support, property division, domestic violence restraining order matters, post-judgment issues, and high-conflict family law disputes.

Wenyu represents individuals navigating financially and emotionally complex transitions, including professionals, dual-income households, parents facing contested custody issues, and clients with significant property or procedural concerns. His work emphasizes careful factual development, thoughtful financial analysis, and practical planning at each phase of a case.

Many Bay Area family law cases involve compensation and property issues that require more than a generic approach. RSUs, options, bonuses, deferred compensation, real property, reimbursements, retirement accounts, and separate property claims can affect both settlement structure and litigation posture. Wenyu approaches these issues with attention to documentation, disclosure obligations, and negotiation leverage.

Before founding his own practice, Wenyu worked as an associate attorney at Law Offices of Rod Firoozye and Wood Smith Henning & Berman LLP, where he gained experience in litigation, motion practice, discovery, case analysis, negotiation, and dispute resolution. Earlier in his legal career, he worked with the California Department of Tax and Fee Administration through the Tax Appeals Assistance Program, assisting taxpayers with administrative tax matters and appeals.

Wenyu earned his Juris Doctor from University of California, Davis School of Law, where he participated in the Business Law Journal and the Asian Pacific American Law Students Association. He received his Bachelor of Arts from University of California, Berkeley.

Clients value an approach that is deliberate rather than reactive. The firm maintains selective intake so each case can receive direct attorney attention, organized communication, and preparation tailored to the facts, deadlines, and practical decisions ahead.
